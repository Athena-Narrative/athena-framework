# Selected References and Conceptual Influences

This repository presents ATHENA as an original analytical articulation.

The references below indicate some of the conceptual traditions that inform its development. ATHENA does not derive directly from any single theory; rather, it integrates insights from multiple research fields including narrative theory, persuasion psychology, cognitive science, and communication studies.

---

# Narrative and Strategic Narratives

Narrative structure and meaning-making are central to how audiences interpret events and actors.

- **Propp, V. (1928)** – *Morphology of the Folktale.*  
  Foundational structural analysis of narrative roles and story patterns.

- **Somers, M. R. (1994)** – *The Narrative Constitution of Identity.*  
  Sociological approach explaining how narratives shape social identity and interpretation.

- **Miskimmon, A., O’Loughlin, B., & Roselle, L. (2013)** – *Strategic Narratives: Communication Power and the New World Order.*  
  Framework for understanding how actors construct narratives that shape political and geopolitical perceptions.

---

# Persuasion, Reactance, and Resistance

ATHENA's **BOUCLIER** dimension draws from research on psychological resistance to persuasion.

- **Brehm, J. W. (1966)** – *A Theory of Psychological Reactance.*  
  Foundational theory explaining how perceived threats to individual freedom produce motivational resistance.

- **Dillard, J. P., & Shen, L. (2005)** – *On the Nature of Reactance and its Role in Persuasive Health Communication.*  
  Empirical operationalization of reactance as a cognitive and affective process involving anger and counter-arguing.

- **Compton, J. (2013)** – *Inoculation Theory.*  
  Research on how exposure to weakened forms of persuasion can strengthen resistance to manipulation.

---

# Cognitive Processing and Persuasion

The **CORPS** and **TERRAIN** dimensions draw from research on cognitive processing and message interpretation.

- **Petty, R. E., & Cacioppo, J. T. (1986)** – *Elaboration Likelihood Model (ELM).*  
  Describes how audiences process persuasive messages through central or peripheral routes.

- **Green, M. C., & Brock, T. C. (2000)** – *Narrative Transportation Theory.*  
  Explains how immersion in a story can reduce counter-arguing and increase persuasion.

- **Tversky, A., & Kahneman, D. (1974)** – *Judgment under Uncertainty: Heuristics and Biases.*  
  Foundational work on cognitive shortcuts and biases influencing decision-making.

---

# Values, Identity, and Audience Context

ATHENA's **TERRAIN** dimension considers how value systems and identity structures influence narrative reception.

- **Schwartz, S. H. (1992)** – *Theory of Basic Human Values.*  
  Framework for understanding cross-cultural value structures.

- **Graves, C. W.; Beck, D.; Cowan, C.** – *Spiral Dynamics.*  
  Developmental model describing evolving value systems and worldviews.

- Literature on **social identity theory**, **motivated reasoning**, and **belief persistence** also informs this dimension.

---

# Behavioral Activation and Communication Dynamics

ATHENA's **VECTEUR** dimension examines the transition from persuasion to action and diffusion.

- **Bandura, A. (1977)** – *Self-Efficacy Theory.*  
  Explains how perceived ability influences behavioral engagement.

- **Gibson, J. J. (1979)** – *Theory of Affordances.*  
  Describes how environments provide opportunities for action.

---

# Information Environments and Narrative Competition

ATHENA is also informed by research on contested information environments and narrative competition.

Relevant traditions include:

- **Strategic communication studies**
- **Disinformation and misinformation research**
- **Influence operations analysis**

Examples include:

- **DISARM framework**
- research on **information warfare and narrative competition**

---

# Complex Systems Perspective

ATHENA can also be interpreted through the lens of complex systems and opinion dynamics.

Relevant research includes work on:

- collective cognition
- belief propagation
- opinion dynamics
- tipping points in social systems

These perspectives help interpret narrative dynamics as **non-linear interactions within a cognitive landscape**.

---

# Note

ATHENA should not be interpreted as a direct derivative of any single theory listed above.

Its contribution lies in the **specific articulation of narrative structure, audience configuration, psychological reactance, and activation dynamics into a unified four-pole analytical framework**.
