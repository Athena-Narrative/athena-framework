# Selected References

This repository presents ATHENA as an original analytical articulation.  
The references below indicate some of the conceptual traditions that inform its development.

## Narrative and strategic narrative
- Propp, V. *Morphology of the Folktale.*
- Somers, M. R. Works on narrative identity and social explanation.
- Miskimmon, A., O’Loughlin, B., & Roselle, L. *Strategic Narratives.*

## Persuasion and reactance
- Petty, R. E., & Cacioppo, J. T. *Elaboration Likelihood Model.*
- Brehm, J. W. *A Theory of Psychological Reactance.*

## Communication, audiences, and belief dynamics
- Research traditions on framing, social identity, motivated reasoning, and belief persistence.
- Literature on resistance to persuasion and defensive processing.

## Information environments
- DISARM framework.
- Strategic communication and disinformation analysis literature.
- Research on contested information environments and narrative competition.

## Complex systems perspective
- Approaches to opinion dynamics, collective cognition, stability, and tipping points in social systems.

---

## Note

ATHENA should not be read as a direct derivative of any one source listed above.

Its contribution lies in the specific articulation of narrative structure, audience configuration, reactance, and activation dynamics into a unified four-pole analytical framework.
